Intel Sustainability Timeline

SUSTAINABILITY THROUGH THE AGES

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1990
First Green Initiative
Intel launches its first company-wide recycling program.
2005
Energy Efficient Chips
Introduction of energy-saving processors for PCs and servers.
2015
Water Conservation
Major investments in water recycling at manufacturing sites.
2025
Net Positive Water

Intel achieves net positive water use, returning more water to communities than it consumes. This milestone is a result of decades of innovation and investment in sustainable manufacturing processes, benefiting both technology and the environment.

2027
AI for Sustainability
Intel leverages AI to optimize energy use in data centers, reducing carbon footprint.
2030
Circular Economy
Major advances in recycling and reusing chip materials, supporting a circular tech economy.
2035
Zero Waste Factories
Intel factories achieve zero waste to landfill, setting a new industry standard.
2040
Global Green Leadership

Intel is recognized as a global leader in green technology, with all operations powered by renewable energy and a positive impact on communities worldwide.
